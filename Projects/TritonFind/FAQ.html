General Questions

How do you work?

Okay, this is how it generally goes down.

  1. You register an item through TritonFind by sending the bot a picture of the item's barcode (usually your student ID)
  2. You accidentally lose your ID. Some generous soul finds it.
  3. They send a picture of the barcode to TritonFind. TritonFind recognizes it as yours, and connects you two through Messenger.
  4. You two talk it out, and find out how to return your item. (see our list of reccommended ways of doing this).
  5. Your item is returned and your day is saved!

Isn't this useless if noone uses it?

Yes, it is. Which is why it is imperative that sign up for it.
Think about it - It takes only about 30 seconds to register your student ID or items with TritionFind. After that, you can ignore it forever. But there may be a time in the future where you desperately need to find your item: maybe Sun God is tomorrow and you need your ID, or you're locked out of your room, or you're scared someone is stealing your dining dollars.
TritonFind will give your mind ease. Sure, you may never use it, but when you absolutely need it, it will be there.

TroubleShooting

How come it's not recognizing my barcode?

In order for TritonFind to find a barcode in a photo you send it, the following must be true:

  1. The picture must be in portrait.
  2. There must be ample lighting.
  3. The picture must be clear, every bar on the barcode must distinguishable.
  4. The barcode should be fairly large.
  5. The barcode must be in the right format.

These are the types of barcode formats that TritonFind accepts (from the zxing website).

  • UPC-A and UPC-E
  • EAN-8 and EAN-13
  • Code 39
  • Code 93
  • Code 128
  • ITF
  • Codabar
  • RSS-14 (all variants)
  • RSS Expanded (most variants)
  • QR Code
  • Data Matrix
  • Aztec ('beta' quality)
  • PDF 417 ('alpha' quality)

Because a majority of TritonFind barcodes are UC San Diego studnet ID's, TritonFind prefers CODABAR barcodes. If you wish to scan something other than a UC San Diego ID, all you have to do is confirm that you are not scanning a UC San Diego ID when taking a picture.

Security/Data Protection

How do I know you won't steal my UCSD PID?

The barcode on the back of a UCSD ID does not coorespond to your actual UCSD PID. There may be an internal list that UCSD keeps to connect those, but TritonFind does not have the ability to do that.
We also reccomend that you take a look at the TritonFind Privacy Policy. We detail exactly how we deal with your potentially secret information.
